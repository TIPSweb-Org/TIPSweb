Toolkit for Illustration of Procedures in Surgery (TIPS)

Overview: TIPS is a collaborative partnership between surgeons and IT researchers from the Dept. of CISE and Dept. of Surgery. The project aims to create a low-cost, computer-based, 3D interactive multimedia authoring and learning environment – complete with force feedback – that places the specialist surgeon at the center of content creation. This innovative tool is designed for publishing, documenting, teaching, and even legal documentation of surgical procedures.

Key People: Dr. Jörg Peters, Minho Kim, Sukitti Punak, Tianyun Ni, Young In Yeo, Ashish Myles, Dr. Juan Cendan, Sergei N. Kurenov

Contact: Dr. Jörg Peters (jorg@cise.ufl.edu)

Research Sponsor: NIH, UF Seed Fund, SurfLab

Website: TIPS Project Page

Evaluation: Evaluation and Validation flow chart (Oct. 30, 2007)

Originality: Unlike existing commercial surgical training tools that focus on fixed tasks like suturing, TIPS empowers specialist surgeons to author and illustrate complex procedures, opening a new channel for communication and education.
